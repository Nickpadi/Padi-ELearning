The Four Central Dive Skills

As you gain dive experience and your skills improve, you become more aware and more confident, and your equipment use becomes second nature. Learning to think like a diver underlies this. Four central skills shape experienced, prudent divers’ thinking:

  1. Planning dives with secondary objectives
  2. Developing and applying situational awareness
  3. Managing task loading
  4. Maintaining good dive habits

These (primarily) mental skills share three characteristics: First, they apply to virtually all dives to some extent. Second, they make your dives more enjoyable and more efficient, and help you reduce and manage risk. Third, they integrate with each other and all your other dive skills.

With foundational knowledge and skills mastered, you’re now ready to transition from novice-thinking to experienced-thinking approaches to diving. No matter how many dives you’ve logged, more training in these skills helps you make the most of your experiences. It structures how you think about diving and lets you benefit from the experience of more seasoned divers.
